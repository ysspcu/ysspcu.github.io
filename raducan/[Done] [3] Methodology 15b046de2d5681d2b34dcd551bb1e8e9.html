Methodology

The methodology chapter outlines the research design, data collection and analysis methods employed in this study to investigate the dynamics of modding community of Minecraft and its impact on player engagement and game development. Given the multifaceted nature of this research, a mixed-methods approach was adopted, integrating both quantitative and qualitative data to provide a comprehensive understanding of the phenomena under study. This chapter details the process involved in survey design, participant selection, data collection, ensuring a thorough exploration of the research questions.

Description of the surveys

The primary data collection instruments were surveys, designed to capture the experiences and perspectives of two key groups: mod users and mod developers. These surveys included a combination of multiple-choices, Likert scale, and open-ended questions, allowing both quantitative and qualitative data collection. These surveys were meticulously crafter to ensure reliability and validity of the data collected. The decision to use surveys was informed by their efficacy in gathering large amounts of data from diverse population in a relatively short time frame (Creswell, 2014). Additionally, a set of interviews were conducted for several mod developers to gather qualitative data, covering more specific area, providing more personalized experiences.

The surveys were divided into several sections, each targeting specific aspects of the participants’ experiences and background. In the initial section, participants were asked to provide general information about their mod usage, including approximate amount of mod usage and the variety of mods they tried. This aimed to quantify the extent of mod engagement among users and developers. Subsequently, the surveys delved into the vanilla Minecraft and mod usage experience, employing Likert scales to assess various factors such as satisfaction with mod quality and quantity, ease of installation, and the overall impact on gameplay. Participants also rated their experiences concerning the enjoyment of modded games, opinions of modded games in general.

The community feedback section explored the frequency and nature of feedback given by users to mod developers. Participants described their experiences providing suggestions for improvements, reporting bugs, and offering general appreciation. This is the most important section as it provides insight into player engagement, highlighting their interactions with mods and their willingness to contribute to the community by providing feedback with clear testing instructions for mod dev to easier debug process. For mod developers, this section aimed to understand the collaborative aspect of the modding community, focusing on peer feedback, code reviews, and sharing of resources and knowledge, highlighting the supportive and cooperative nature of the modding community.

In the final section, participants provided general information about themselves, including their computer proficiency level, age, and gender. Participants rated their computer skills on a scale from beginner to advanced, which provided context to their modding experiences and helped correlate technical proficiency with mod usage and development patterns. Demographic questions about age, gender ensured a diverse and representative sample, allowing for the examination of trends across different demographic groups.

For the qualitative part a set of questions were provided to a smaller group of mod developers. These interview questions were designed to delve deeply into the experiences, motivations and challenges faced by mod developers involved in creating Minecraft Mods. These questions aim to capture a comprehensive understanding of their creative process, skills development, community interactions and the broader impact of their work on both personal and professional levels. The interviews provided rich qualitative data, allowing for nuanced insights into the modding community.

The interview began by exploring participants’ interest and inspiration for making Minecraft mods, including how they first became interested in modding and where they draw their inspiration for mod ideas. This section aimed to uncover the initial spark that led to their modding journey and the sources of creativity that fuel their projects. The next focus was on the creative process, asking participants how they turn an idea into a final product and the typical duration of completing a mod. This section provided insights into the workflow and methodologies employed by mod developers, highlighting the balance between creativity and technical execution. To understand how mod developers overcome obstacles, questions about overcoming challenges were included. Participants shared their strategies for dealing with creative blocks or technical issues, revealing the resilience and problem-solving skills crucial for successful modding.

The skills and background section examined prior knowledge or experience that helped participants when they started making mods. It also explored the new skills they had to acquire and the methods they used to learn them. This section highlighted the learning curve and the resourcefulness required in the modding community. Questions about first struggles and learning focused on the initial difficulties faced by participants and how they overcame these challenges. This provided a window into the perseverance and adaptability necessary for modding. The interviews also delved into project-based learning, asking which projects required participants to learn new skills and what specific skills were refined with their latest mod. This section underscored the continuous learning aspect of modding, where each project serves as a learning opportunity.

For insights into the complexity of their work, participants discussed their most complicated or difficult modding projects, detailing the processes and challenges encountered. This highlighted the technical and creative demands of high-level modding. The mod usage in Minecraft section explored the types of mods participants personally use in their Minecraft game and the essential mods they cannot play without. This aimed to identify personal preferences and how they influence their mod development. Questions on the impact on gameplay perspective examined how modding experiences alter participants' views of Minecraft and their perception of the original product. This provided insights into the transformative effect of modding on player engagement and game perception.

The interviews also addressed videogame preferences, exploring how modding influences participants' overall preferences in video games. This section aimed to capture the broader impact of modding on gaming habits and preferences. Participants were asked for feedback to Mojang, expressing their thoughts on the game's development direction, including what they appreciate and what they wish to change. This provided a direct line of feedback from modders to the game's developers. Questions about game versions and porting examined how different versions of Minecraft affect mod development and the challenges associated with creating or porting mods for new versions. This section highlighted the technical challenges and adaptation required in response to game updates.

The game updates section focused on experiences with mods breaking due to updates and how participants responded to such setbacks. This provided insights into the resilience and adaptability required in the modding community. Participants shared their views on the monetization of mods, discussing their opinions on charging for mod content and the ethical implications of commercialization in the modding space. Copyright issues were addressed, with participants sharing any experiences with copyright conflicts and their opinions on how these situations were handled. This section highlighted the legal challenges faced by mod developers. The modding community questions asked participants to describe the Minecraft modding community, focusing on behaviors and practices they value or dislike. This provided an understanding of the social dynamics within the community.

Questions about interactions and status explored the frequency and nature of interactions with other community members and any perceived symbols of status or influence within the community. This aimed to understand the social hierarchy and engagement levels in the modding space. The influence and indicators section investigated what gives someone influence in the modding community and the indicators that suggest expertise on modding forums. This provided insights into the recognition and respect mechanisms within the community. Collaboration and community influence questions focused on experiences with collaborative projects, examining how these interactions influence participants' motivation and skill development. This highlighted the collaborative spirit and knowledge-sharing culture in modding. Lastly, newbies and knowledge application questions assessed participants' attitudes towards newcomers in the modding space and any advice they have offered. It also explored how the skills learned from modding are applicable outside the modding context, providing a broader perspective on the transferable skills gained through modding. These interviews were designed to capture a rich tapestry of experiences and insights from mod developers, contributing to a deeper understanding of the modding community and its impact on both individual participants and the broader gaming ecosystem.

Participant selection

Participants for the surveys were selected through both purposive and convenience samplings, targeting individuals actively engaged in modding community. This approach was chosen to ensure that the sample was representative of the broader modding population, thereby enhancing the generalizability of the findings (Etikan, Musa, & Alkassim, 2016).

Purposive sampling was employed to specifically target individuals who are known to have substantial involvement in modding, such as active contributors on modding forums, experienced mod developers and frequent mod users. This method ensured that the participants had the necessary experience and knowledge to provide insightful responses relevant to the study objectives.

In addition, convenience was utilized to recruit a broader range of participants who may be less prominently involved in modding, but still contribute to and engage with the community. This approach helped to capture a wider spectrum of experiences and perspectives, making the findings more inclusive and comprehensive.

Data collection and analysis methods

Data collections was conducted using the online tool provided by Google, called Google Forms, for both general surveys and individual interviews, providing an efficient and accessible means of reaching participants globally. Participants were recruited through various online platforms popular among the modding community. These included social media communities and GitHub. Social media communities on platforms like Reddit and Discord offered access to large, active groups of modders and mod users who engaged in real-time discussions and collaborations. GitHub was particularly used for reaching mod developers who host and share their projects on this platform.

Quantitative data were analyzed using descriptive and inferential statistical methods. Descriptive statistics, such as means, medians, modes, and standard deviations provided an overview of general trends in mod usage and player engagement. Frequency distributions helper visualize the distribution of responses. Inferential statistics, including t-tests and ANOVA, were employed to compare different groups within the sample, mod users versus mod developers, to determine if there were statistically significant differences in their responses. The chi-square test was used to explore the relationships between categorical variables, like mod usage frequency and satisfaction levels. Pearson correlation aws implemented to examine the relationship time spent modding and overall satisfaction. Multiple regression analysis was used to predict player engagement based on factors like mod usage, age, and computer proficiency.

Qualitative data from the interviews were analyzed using thematic analysis. The process began with familiarization, where data were transcribed and read multiple times to identify initial patterns. This was followed by coding, where meaningful data segments were labeled. These codes were then collated into themes, which were reviewed and refined to ensure they accurately captured the participants’ experiences and perspectives. Finally, the themes were defined and named, resulting in a coherent narrative that reflects the core insights of the qualitative data.

Community feedback, gathered though the survey’s dedicated section was crucial for understanding player engagement. By analyzing the frequency and nature of feedback provided by users, insights were gained into how players interact with mods and their willingness to contribute to the community. The feedback loop highlighted the collaborative nature of modding community and underscored the importance of user-generated content in enhancing experiences and driving game development.

Ethical considerations were paramount throughout the research process, with the participants informed of the study’s purpose, assured of their anonymity, and given the option to get involved or refuse at any time.

By combining these methods, the research leveraged the strengths of both quantitative and qualitative analysis to gain a comprehensive and nuanced understanding of the modding community and its impact on player engagement and game development.
